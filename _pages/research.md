My research interests lie at the intersection of applied microeconomics, behavioral economics, and public policy.

I am interested in understanding how individuals make economic decisions and how institutions and policies shape social and economic outcomes.

My current interests include:

- Behavioral and Experimental Economics
- Applied Microeconomics
- Public Policy
- Labor Economics
- Economic Decision-Making

I have experience working with survey data and empirical methods using R, Python, and Stata.
